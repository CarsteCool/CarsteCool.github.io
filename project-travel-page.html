Travel to Seattle

Places you should go

    Space Needle

    Check out the Space Needle while driving on I-5, just to the east of the skyscrapers.
    The Space Needle was originally designed by Edward Carlson and built for temporary use at the 1962 World Fair.

    Mount Rainier

    While not actually in Seattle, Mount Rainier is still a great sight to go see. Mount Rainier is mainly impressive because it is so much larger than the surrounding Cascade Mountains. This is because Mount Rainier is actually a dormant volcano.

    Pike Place Market

    If you go to the Pike Place Market, you can see workers preparing orders of fish by throwing the fish to each other.
    The workers turn their busness into a show by asking people to gather around the counter and throwing many large fish back and forth.

Other info

  1. Seattle is home to the Seattle Seahawks, the professional football team from the area.

  2. Seattle is home to the world's longest floating bridge, the Evergreen Point Bridge.
    The bridge is on Washington State Route 520.

  3. Microsoft Game Studios and Nintendo were originally headquartered in Redmond, WA, just 15 miles from Seattle.
